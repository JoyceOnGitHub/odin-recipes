Homemade Fried Rice

Hungry and only have scraps at your home? This homemade arroz chaufa recipe is quick and easy to make on those days where cooking is just too much.

Arroz chaufa is a peruvian fried rice that is both modular and easy to make.

You can whip this up with your meat of choice and no matter what it'll still bring perfection to your taste buds!

Ingredients

  • Meat of any choice
  • Soy sauce
  • Oil (vegetable, olive, or corn works)
  • Toasted sesame oil
  • MSG (monosodium glutamate)
  • Leftover(or fresh) rice
  • 1-2 Eggs
  • Chives or green onions (both optional)
  • Goya adobo seasoning (Optional, you can use your seasoning of choice on meat)
  • Butter
  • Salt

Recipe Steps

  1. Pick any meat of your choice to cook. I preferably enjoy using steak but chicken and pork work really well too! Before you cook your meat, cut it into small cube-like chunks. These chunks will mix with rice really well.
  2. Pour some oil into your pan and let your oil heat up on a medium flame.
  3. Once ready, pour your chunks in, sprinkle some adobo seasoning or your seasoning of choice.
  4. Let the meat cook till done.
  5. Grab another plate and remove the cooked chunks of meat off the pan.
  6. Now add butter to the pan and let it heat up.
  7. Crack 1-2 eggs and season with salt.
  8. Move the eggs around, you want them scrambled.
  9. Lower heat to a medium, leave the eggs in the pan and pour in your rice.
  10. Move it around and work it.
  11. The rice is warming up now, go on and add a quick pour of toasted sesame oil, about ½ tablespoon.
  12. Move it around and then sprinkle a little bit of soy sauce around the rice. (Note: Too much soy sauce can make it very salty so be conservative with it.)
  13. Taste the rice, it should be hot but not tongue-burning hot.
  14. Now add in your chunks of meat, move it around.
  15. You can turn off the flame and add a dash or two of MSG.
  16. Once more, move it around.
  17. You can plate it and optionally sprinkle some chives or green onions on top!
  18. You’re done! Now sit back and enjoy that chaufa!
